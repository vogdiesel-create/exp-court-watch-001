Why Smart Investors Monitor Court Filings

When a major class action lawsuit is filed against a public company, the stock often drops 5-15% within days. But the filing itself is public record -- usually available on PACER hours or days before it hits the financial news.

The Information Edge

PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) publishes federal court filings in near real-time. The problem is that PACER is designed for lawyers, not investors. Searching it effectively requires knowing case numbers, court jurisdictions, and legal terminology.

What Automated Monitoring Catches

  • New lawsuits filed against companies in your portfolio
  • SEC enforcement actions and investigations
  • Patent infringement cases that could affect revenue
  • Settlements and judgments that impact balance sheets
  • Class action filings that precede stock drops
